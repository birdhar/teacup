TEA: Pride of India

Tea is the most popular manufactured drink consumed in the world, equaling all others – including coffee, soft drinks, and alcohol – combined.
India is one of the largest producer and consumer of tea in the world. It stands among top 4 countries in terms of tea export after Kenya (including neighbouring African countries), China and Sri Lanka. Here, it is famously known as 'chai'. Although it is one of the largest tea producers in the world, over 70 percent of its tea is consumed within India itself. The tea produced in India is among the finest in the world owing to strong geographical indications, heavy investments in tea processing units, continuous innovation, augmented product mix and strategic market expansion.
North East India is known as major tea plantation states along with Himachal Pradesh. Sikkim and tea garden of Tripura are known for its perfect taste and aroma. It is home to a wide variety of teas, including CTC tea, orthodox tea, green tea and organic tea. Unlike many other teas producing and exporting nations, India has a manufacturing base for both CTC and orthodox tea in addition to green tea.
India has a significant share in the international tea market with around 12 per cent share of world tea exports. Russia, the UAE, the UK, Iran, Kazakhstan, the US, Egypt, Afghanistan, Germany, Pakistan, Australia, Sri Lanka, Poland and Iraq are some of the major global markets for exports of Indian tea.

COFFEE

Coffee is one of the world’s most popular drink. It is native to tropical Africa and is grown in more than 70 countries in the equatorial regions of the Americas, Africa, the Indian subcontinent, and Southeast Asia. India is home to over 16 unique indigenous coffee varietals
Coffee is grown in three regions of India with Karnataka, Kerala and Tamil Nadu forming the traditional coffee growing region, followed by the new areas developed in the non-traditional areas of Andhra Pradesh and Odisha in the eastern coast of the country and with a third region comprising the states of Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Tripura, Nagaland and Arunachal Pradesh
India is the third-largest producer and exporter of coffee in Asia, and the sixth-largest producer and fifth-largest exporter of coffee in the world which accounts for 3.14% (2019-20) of the global coffee production.

SPICES

A spice is a seed, fruit, root, bark, or other plant substance primarily used for flavoring or coloring food. It play a significant role in the way we cook and consume food around the world. There are many spices we use in our day to day cooking, these include popular ones like cumin, black pepper, turmeric and coriander seeds etc.
India known as the home of spices, produces a wide range of spices and holds a prominent position in world spice production. the country produces about 75 of the 109 varieties listed by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and accounts for half of the global trading in spices. Demand of Indian spices is increasing due to its unique aroma, texture, and flavour.
Spices worth US$ 3.65 billion were exported from India in the financial year 2020, which was US$ 2.80 billion in the year 2019 and US$ 2.78 billion in the year 2018. Therefore, we see that the export of Indian spices registered an increase of 7% in volume.
